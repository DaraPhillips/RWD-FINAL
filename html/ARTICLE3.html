ABOUT IT

The best of the best

If you ask any surfer to name a list of the most famous waves of all, they'll more than likely mention Supertubos.
Supertubos is an incredible beach break situated in Peniche, Portugal.
This fishing village is home to long, hollow, and deep barrels that are produced scarily close to the beach.
As you can imagine it has huge tourist attraction value that draws in crowds and surfers from all over the globe to get a chance at catching a beautiful tube like wave or to watch the pros in action.
The funny thing is although it's a beach break it has the right conditons to create the type of waves surfers dream of, without the dangers of hitting a rocky floor like Hawaii's Banzai Pipeline. The reason for this stems from a deep underwater canyon that creates more energy and pushes it towards the beach.
Unlike other breaks, it can be surfed in all tide conditions. A mid to high tide, with West swells and light NorthEast wind is ideal.
Although it may be a beach break, due to the heavy and demanding nature of the waves, it's strickly reccommended that only experienced surfers attempt surfing at Supertubos. Patience, confidence, commitment and skills to master a steep drop are all neccessary here. Analysing and planning is also something you should do before taking to the water.
The best time of year to surf Supertubos is from September to November, so bringing a 3/2mm or 4/3mm wetsuit is a good idea.
