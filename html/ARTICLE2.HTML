World Record Wave

In this world, some people are born different. They cannot help but to do things that most other people may think is a nightmare. For the dare devils, the fear keeps them focused. It makes them feel alive and sucks them in to an endless cycle of wanting more of a rush, and I am with them! One thing that can induce said rush is surfing, BIG WAVE SURFING.
Surfing, like all other things in this world is complex and it can be broken down into various subsets. There are nuances throughout the sport, and it takes time to get to understand the distinct aspects. These aspects include but are not limited to, the type of breaks, the types of waves, the type of wind direction, the type of board... you get the idea, there is a learning curve.
For some, surfing in small waves and learning to do tricks is all it is about, however, some dare to be great and to go where no person has gone before. On the 29th of October 2020 in Nazar√©, Portugal, a German born professional big wave surfer by the name of Sebastian Steudtner did just that. The man did not just surf a big wave for all of you rolling your eyes. Both Sebastian and his team risked their lives to print their names in the history books and look pretty cool at the same time. This one ride will continue to inspire surfers of all ages globally that anything is possible.
