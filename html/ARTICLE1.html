Old Dog, New Tricks

They say old dogs can't learn new tricks. What I want to know is, who the hell even are they?
Being introduced and falling in love with sports is a bittersweet venture. One never believes for a second that their passion could die or, even trying to picture themselves doing something else can be an impossible and unfathomable task.
Unfortunately, there comes a time in most sports enthusiast's lives when enough is enough and it's time to call it a day. Generally it's the person's body that can no longer perform at the required level or worse at any level.
Marco (44) was a professional soccer player in Portugal. Throughout his life he didn't have much time for other hobbies. Training for soccer was always a priority. He held an extensive career, and when it was over, he didn't know where to turn.
Now faced with a lot of free time, he spent his evenings walking his dog Max (10) along the beach near their home. Every evening they would walk to a particular bench and rest, watching the surfers catch waves in the distance.
Marco had never surfed, and one day he decided it was something he wanted to try. One morning on a beach in Portugal he plucked up the courage and went to a surf shop to rent a board and a wetsuit.
He quickly realised that he had a big problem on his hands. As soon as he touched the water, Max would let out a yelp and run to his aid. He had a choice to make. Lock Max up at home or let him try too.
This picture is taken 2 years on. Marco and Max are now the highlight of every person who visits their local beach.
It may be 'just a proverb' but, you're never too old to learn anything if you really want to and you can teach old dogs new tricks...
